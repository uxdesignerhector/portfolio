Silence at Magma

2017 November (a week)

Main role: Graphic Designer

Problem

A lot of calls

When I arrived at Magma there were a lot of calls between employees and external clients due to the idiosyncrasy of the sector. Even me, as designer, I had to attend calls when the Magma switchboard was occupied. There were also new employees who arrived at Magma offices who didn't know which were the phone numbers or the emails of each department.

Awful contact card

To solve all these problems there were a printed excell contact table, but it was so ominous that it was impossible to read it. It was really tiny, making impossible to find it out and the most hilarious part, it was a photocopy of a photocopy of a printed table so every text and data were blurred.

Consecuences

All this facts caused that when a call was being attended and needed to be pass to another team mate, they had to ask for the phone number in a loud voice (this also happened with emails, some departments had different terminations between their employees, even if they were the same department .com or .net). Sometimes this occurred when meetings were taking place, departments were debating solutions or clients were waiting in the hall for meetings, generating noise when silence was needed. So I decided to bring a new solution, a new contact card.

Requirements

  • The new contact card should be:
    • Easy to read
    • Easy to find
    • Fast reading and detection
  • The data it should have:
    • Emails (required)
    • Phone numbers (required)
    • Name of the employee (required)
    • Department (optional)
  • Conditions:
    • A lot of data to be printed in a small piece
    • Printed technology
    • Not enough space at employees tables

Architecture information

Wich order should follow the data?

At first, I thought it should be ok to order the data by department but trough analysis I found out that it was irrelevant to the employees, that data order was no a real solution, since they remembered each team mate name and which department they belong to.

Other approach was to take the importance of the employee in the organization so the CEO and high rank employees should be the first in the contact card, but with this option fast reading was not feasible.

The final solution was to take an alphabetical order, it was easy and fast to find names and since a lot of calls would ask for names employees it was the very first piece of data that receptor would received and it should be the first to appear at the contact card to make the faster decission.

How to make an easy finding?

The tables of workers were full of white papers and the walls full of post it, photos and calendars so I needed to make an obvious finding item, it also needed to be small to not downplay other information. I decided to contrast it the most against the white and black colour of papers that were ruling the space so I chose a red background color for the contact card.

Solution

What should be changed?

Round corners: in the name of safety I made round corners but they are handicap when you print them, because you need to cut them, making you to lose time and if there where a change you must print again and replace the old contact cards.

Red background: sometimes a lot of red can be disturbing, it also affects the readability of the data especially if it is black and you have a small font. Today I will leave the background colour in white and maybe develop a different approach with the red. It is also a handicap when printing because it makes you lose time while the printer is filling all background with red ink.

What did I got?

The design worked on his purpose and the day I gave the contact card to everybody they stopped asking for phone numbers and emails. Also some employees pasted it on to their monitors or their walls so they could read it directly. And that way is how I got a bit of silence at the offices.
