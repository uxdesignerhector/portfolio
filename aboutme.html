My value

I understood that design can change behaviours when I designed a printed contact card for my team mates at Magma and they stopped asking for their phones and emails in a loud voice, since that day I always pursued this objective through my designs.

I'm always looking for unexpected ideas to solve problems. Expect from me to anticipate to errors. When I was working at Magma, I had to learn how to approach a project in the most efficient way since I was the only designer and projects would concatenate each other.

Also there is always time for a good joke.

Why UX?

During my time at Magma I took interest in how their employees were interacting with their environment and how work processes affected their emotions.

I realised there were a lot of things that could be changed. For example, the process of hiring new employees required new contracts to be sealed by the only one human resources employee at Madrid. There could be about 30 new contracts each week to be signed that implied a total number of 180 legal papers to be sealed by hand, consuming a vast amount of time. I asked this employee how this problem could be solve and she answered there were print machines that do this automatically! Therefore why not to change this method to and effortless an automatic one? Maybe it was an economic handicap or a decision made by the company. As I was the designer I couldn't change things like that, but I understood that the overall emotions and feeling affect productivity of employees and clients decisions equally.
